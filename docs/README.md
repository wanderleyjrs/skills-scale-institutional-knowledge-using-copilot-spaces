# OctoAcme Project Management Docs — README

Welcome to the OctoAcme Project Management documentation hub! This repository consolidates all core practices, templates, and guides used by OctoAcme to run cross-functional projects—from initiation to delivery, release, and retrospective.

---

## Project Management Processes Summary

OctoAcme uses a structured, lifecycle-based approach to project management that emphasizes customer value, iterative delivery, and clear ownership. The methodology spans five distinct phases: **Initiation**, where business needs are validated and stakeholders aligned through a lightweight Project One-pager; **Planning**, where work is broken into shippable increments with prioritized backlogs and risk registers; **Execution**, where teams deliver through daily standups and structured PR workflows; **Release**, with standardized deployment checklists and rollback protocols; and **Close & Retrospective**, where learnings are captured and converted into continuous improvements. This phased approach ensures that projects start with validated success metrics and proceed with clear visibility and control.

The organization defines clear roles and responsibilities to enable effective collaboration. **Project Managers (PMs)** coordinate delivery schedules, manage risks, and facilitate communication across stakeholders. **Product Managers (PdMs)** define outcomes, prioritize backlogs, and measure impact. **Developers** implement features, write tests, and collaborate on design decisions. **QA/Testing** teams validate quality and acceptance criteria. This clarity of ownership, combined with a principle of psychological safety and data-informed decisions, creates an environment where cross-functional teams can move quickly while maintaining alignment.

Communication and risk management are woven throughout OctoAcme's execution model. Teams operate on a regular cadence: daily standups (15 minutes) focus on progress and blockers, weekly delivery syncs track milestone progress and risks, and monthly stakeholder updates keep sponsors informed. A three-level escalation path—from team triage through PM to Product Lead and finally sponsor—ensures that blockers are surfaced and resolved appropriately. Risk registers are maintained throughout the project lifecycle with clear identification, assessment, mitigation plans, and status tracking.

Quality and delivery standards are enforced through both process discipline and technical controls. All work follows a Definition of Done that includes unit tests, integration tests, end-to-end smoke tests for critical flows, and security scanning in CI pipelines. Pull requests are kept small (≤400 lines when possible), require at least one approval, and include issue links and acceptance criteria in descriptions. Metrics such as velocity, burndown, and business success indicators are monitored via dashboards. This combination of rigorous testing, small iterative releases, and continuous measurement enables OctoAcme to deliver value reliably while learning and improving with each cycle.
